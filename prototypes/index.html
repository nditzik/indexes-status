How to use

  • Open each mockup in a browser at 1440p (or any modern monitor — design adapts down to 1366px).
  • The mockup-controls panel at bottom-left lets you toggle: Light theme, Sidebar collapse, Rail collapse, collapsible sections.
  • All values are realistic sample data reflecting a "cautiously constructive" market state.
  • Sparklines and charts are SVG placeholders showing the design intent — not live Chart.js.
  • Hover on any panel/KPI to see the border/background transition. Hover on heatmap cells for scale effect.
  • Sidebar navigation between mockups works — click Overview / Internals / Sectors in the sidebar.

Visual grammar checklist

  • EYEBROW · category color · uppercase 10px · +0.08em letter-spacing → above every panel title.
  • Card structure: 2px accent top-border · eyebrow · title · subtitle · divider · body · divider · footer (1-line interpretation).
  • Tabular numbers: JetBrains Mono with font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums on all metrics.
  • State color (pos/neg/warn) decoupled from category color (breadth/momentum/risk/flow/sector/macro/historical/action).
  • Spacing: 4px base · 16px cards · 24px between sections · 8px tight (KPI grid).
  • Hover: 120ms ease · border darkens, optional bg shift, no shadows.

What to validate

  • Density: dense but readable? Or still cramped / loose?
  • Hierarchy: does the eye flow MCC → Score panels → KPIs → Snapshot row naturally?
  • Category color usage: feels professional, or too subtle/too loud?
  • 1440p fit: does each tab fit without scrolling much? (Overview: yes. Internals: scattered. Sectors: scroll needed for trends.)
  • Light theme: feels balanced, or just inverted-dark?
  • Sidebar collapse: usable as icon-only? Or needs labels?
  • Hebrew + LTR numbers: read naturally or feel mixed?
